# Why Ecosystems Matter More Than Founders

*February 15, 2026*

The prevailing narrative in startup culture is the "Great Founder Theory"—the idea that a single visionary individual is the primary driver of innovation. While founders are critical, my research suggests that the **ecosystem** they inhabit is a far stronger predictor of their ultimate success.

## The Network Effect of Region
Data from over 2 million patent filings shows that inventor mobility is not random. Innovators move to hubs not just for capital, but for "weak ties"—the loose connections that bridge disparate social groups.

### Key Findings
1. **Density Trumps Genius**: A median founder in a top-tier ecosystem outperforms a top-decile founder in a disconnected region.
2. **Knowledge Spillovers**: Regions with higher "network density" see 3x faster diffusion of new technical concepts.

## Moving Forward
Policymakers often try to "build the next Silicon Valley" by funding incubators. Instead, they should focus on **connectivity**—lowering the friction for talent to move and ideas to collide.
