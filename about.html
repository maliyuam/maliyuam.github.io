Background

Professional Biography

I'm the bridge between big ideas and tangible outcomes, with a background in technology, entrepreneurship, and design that prioritizes People, Planet, and Profit. I thrive on tackling complex projects, bringing structure to chaos, and ensuring that every initiative delivers value on multiple levels.

My approach is straightforward: find the best path forward, adapt when necessary, and deliver outcomes that create meaningful impact. I focus on building strong communication channels and creating a shared vision, so each person is aligned and engaged. For me, success is a team that feels empowered and valued in every project.

At Carnegie Mellon University, I'm pursuing a Master's in Information Technology while conducting research on AI's application in Entrepreneurship. Similarly, Muhammad is exploring global AI governance frameworks and their use or lack of in Africa. I'm particularly interested in how AI can catalyze growth in developing markets and contribute to Africa's role in the global economy.

As the founding president of the Africa Innovators Society, I led the creation of a vibrant ecosystem to foster student entrepreneurship and innovation at CMU-Africa. This included organizing workshops, hackathons, and bootcamps that impacted over 300 students across seven universities, while securing over $17,000 in event partnerships.

My career mission is to develop sustainable businesses, impact-driven programs and strategies with a future-facing approach. My decisions consider sustainability—whether that's minimizing waste, optimizing resources, or designing processes that reduce environmental impact. For me, a project's success isn't just about immediate results; it's about long-term responsibility.
